Why It Matters

Histology — the microscopic study of tissue structure — reveals how cells are organized into functional units. Recognizing the four major tissue types (epithelial, connective, muscle, and nervous) under a microscope is a foundational skill for understanding how multicellular organisms are built and how their systems function.
