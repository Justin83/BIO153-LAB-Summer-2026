Why It Matters

Porifera (sponges) and Cnidaria (jellyfish, hydra, corals) represent two of the earliest-diverging animal lineages and illustrate fundamental concepts in animal body organization: from the absence of true tissues in sponges to the radial symmetry and cnidocytes of cnidarians. These groups set the stage for the more complex animal phyla studied in Week 4.
