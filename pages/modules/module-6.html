Population Growth and Competition

ModuleBIO153 Week 6
DueJuly 27
PlatformConnect vLab

Synopsis

In this simulation, you will investigate the classic competition experiment using two freshwater protists, Paramecium caudatum and Paramecium aurelia. The two species are grown separately and together so that you can compare how each population changes when it has access to resources alone versus when both species compete for the same food supply.

You will examine samples at regular intervals, count cells on wet-mount slides, convert those counts to population density in cells per milliliter, and graph the results across the 16-day experiment. The completed growth curves will allow you to identify exponential and logistic growth, estimate carrying capacity, compare intraspecific and interspecific competition, and evaluate the competitive exclusion principle.

Lab To-Do List

  • 1Open the Population Biology — Growth and Competition vLab through Blackboard. Review exponential growth, logistic growth, carrying capacity, ecological niche, and competitive exclusion before entering the simulation.
  • 2Form a prediction for what will happen when P. caudatum and P. aurelia are grown in separate cultures and when they are placed together in a mixed culture.
  • 3Observe and distinguish the two protists: P. caudatum is generally more elongated, while P. aurelia is smaller and rounder.
  • 4Count the organisms in each 0.5-mL wet-mount sample. Convert each count to cells per milliliter by multiplying the observed count by two, then record the result in the lab data table.
  • 5Follow the populations at two-day intervals across the 16-day experiment and create growth curves for each species grown alone and in the mixed culture.
  • 6Compare the curves. Identify where rapid growth slows, where a carrying capacity is reached, and how competition changes the population density of each species.
  • 7Use the mixed-culture results to evaluate whether the two species can occupy the same niche indefinitely and explain which species is the stronger competitor under these conditions.
