Introduction

Welcome to BIO 153: Principles of Biology Laboratory II. This online laboratory course includes organismal, population, and community biology. It supports the BIO 152 lecture course by giving you structured lab practice with taxonomy, organismal diversity, animal tissues, virtual dissections, population ecology, and ecosystem sampling.

In this section, you will learn how the lab course is organized, how to navigate Blackboard, and how to reach me. You will also review key course tools, weekly lab expectations, and communication guidelines.

Why it matters: This section covers the essential lab course information you will use from day one.

How you will use this: You will use this information to navigate the course, access McGraw Hill virtual labs, complete Blackboard Lab Wrap-Up quizzes, and submit your work on time.

Finding Work in the Class

Once you open a module, move through the pages in order by selecting the arrow on the right side of the module page. You can also use the arrow on the left to return to a previous page.

Each module includes information and activities that help you reach the week’s lab objectives. It is important to go through each page in a module because many pages explain a lab assignment before you complete it. Do not skip directly to the graded activities.

You are required to access all course materials through Blackboard. Begin and end all assignments in Blackboard. Many lab assignments will transfer you to McGraw Hill Connect. When you finish, choose the option to return to Blackboard so your grades transfer properly.

Online Etiquette

This video provides an overview of online etiquette, including good practices for online communication in messages, discussion forums, and virtual meetings.

Please practice the following guidelines when posting comments or communicating in the course:

  • Format your text so it is easy to read. Use standard paragraph spacing and legible font choices.
  • Be concise and to the point. Keep discussion posts focused and proofread before posting.
  • Avoid the use of all caps. All caps may be interpreted as shouting and can make text more difficult to read.
  • Avoid informal abbreviations such as OMG in academic course communication.
  • Avoid humor and sarcasm when tone may be difficult to detect in written communication.
  • Practice civil discussion. Flaming, insults, or hostile comments are not appropriate in this course.
  • Respect the privacy of communication within the course. Do not publicly forward classmates’ comments outside of the course.
  • Stay on topic in discussions and course messages.
  • Be respectful. Share comments in ways that are not likely to be misinterpreted, and provide constructive suggestions privately when appropriate.
