Introduction

Welcome to BIO 153: Principles of Biology Laboratory II. This online lab course emphasizes organismal, population, and community biology through virtual labs, histology, organismal diversity activities, and dissection-related lab work. It is designed as a companion to BIO 152 and may be taken concurrently or after completing BIO 152.

In this section, you will learn how the lab course is organized, where to find assignments, and how to reach me. Most assignments are completed through McGraw Hill Connect virtual labs.

Why it matters: Understanding the course structure from the start will help you locate assignments, manage due dates, and stay on track.

Note: Some assignment records in this course are incomplete — point values or time estimates may show as TBD where data is not yet available. Check Blackboard and Connect for the authoritative version.

Finding Lab Work

Most lab assignments are accessed through McGraw Hill Connect, launched from inside Blackboard. Always begin and end your session in Blackboard so your grades transfer correctly.

Exercise assignments (numbered exercises such as Exercise 36–41) may require you to complete PDF-based or printable lab activities as directed in Blackboard. Check the assignment page in Blackboard for specific instructions.
