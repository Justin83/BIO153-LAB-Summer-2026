Introduction

Welcome to BIO 153: Principles of Biology Laboratory II. This online lab course emphasizes organismal, population, and community biology through virtual labs, histology activities, organismal diversity exercises, and dissection activities. It is the companion lab course to BIO 152 and requires BIO 152 as a prerequisite or concurrent enrollment.

In this section, you will learn how the course is organized, how to navigate Blackboard, how to access Connect virtual labs, and how to reach me.

Why it matters: Lab courses reinforce lecture concepts through hands-on (virtual) practice. Understanding how to navigate and submit your lab work correctly is essential from the start.

How you will use this: You will use this information to locate and submit lab assignments on time, every week.

Submitting Lab Work

Most lab assignments are completed through McGraw Hill Connect, accessed from within Blackboard. Always start and finish each lab session from Blackboard so that your grades transfer correctly to the Blackboard Gradebook.

For virtual labs and dissection activities, follow all on-screen instructions within Connect. If you encounter a technical issue, contact McGraw Hill Connect support and notify me via Blackboard Messages.
