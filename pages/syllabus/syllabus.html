Course Description

BIO 153 Principles of Biology Laboratory II includes organismal, population, and community biology. It is the laboratory companion to the BIO 152 lecture course.

BIO 153 is delivered as an online laboratory course that supports organismal, population, community, and ecosystem biology. Students complete virtual laboratory assignments, short Blackboard Lab Wrap-Up quizzes, and a final lab exam. The course begins with taxonomy and phylogeny, then moves through protists, fungi, plants, animal tissues, animal anatomy, population ecology, and ecosystem sampling.

Course Competencies

Upon completion of this course, the student can:

  1. Demonstrate the basic use of taxonomic keys.
  2. Describe the structure, function, evolutionary relationships, and classification of organisms.
  3. Explain the relationships between organisms and their environment.
  4. Apply major biological concepts to real-life settings.

Course Topic Outline

BIO 153 is organized as an online laboratory course that supports organismal, population, community, and ecosystem biology. Each week pairs a McGraw Hill virtual lab assignment with a short Blackboard Lab Wrap-Up quiz, building toward a cumulative final lab exam.

Week 1: Taxonomy, Phylogeny, and Cladistics

  • McGraw Hill Lab: Evidence of Evolution – Phylogeny and Cladistics
  • Blackboard: Lab Wrap-Up Quiz 1
  • Focus: taxonomic reasoning, cladistics, evolutionary relationships, and reading phylogenetic trees

Week 2: Protists and Eukaryotic Diversity

  • McGraw Hill Lab: Organismal Diversity – Protists
  • Blackboard: Lab Wrap-Up Quiz 2
  • Focus: protist diversity, morphology, classification, and eukaryotic evolution

Week 3: Fungi and Fungal Diversity

  • McGraw Hill Lab: Organismal Diversity – Fungi
  • Blackboard: Lab Wrap-Up Quiz 3
  • Focus: fungal classification, morphology, physiology, reproduction, and ecological roles

Week 4: Plant Diversity and Reproduction

  • McGraw Hill Labs: Plants – Mosses and Ferns; Plants – Gymnosperms
  • Blackboard: Lab Wrap-Up Quiz 4
  • Focus: major plant groups, plant reproduction, and plant evolutionary diversity

Week 5: Animal Tissues and Histology

  • McGraw Hill Labs: Connective Tissue Histology; Epithelial Tissue Histology; Muscle Tissue Histology; Nervous Tissue Histology
  • Blackboard: Lab Wrap-Up Quiz 5
  • Focus: major animal tissue types and the relationship between tissue structure and function

Week 6: Animal Diversity and Vertebrate Anatomy I

  • McGraw Hill Labs: Organismal Diversity – Sponges and Cnidarians; Fetal Pig Part 1
  • Blackboard: Lab Wrap-Up Quiz 6
  • Focus: animal diversity, basic animal classification, and vertebrate digestive, respiratory, and endocrine anatomy

Week 7: Vertebrate Anatomy II and Population Ecology

  • McGraw Hill Labs: Fetal Pig Part 2; Population Biology – Growth and Competition
  • Blackboard: Lab Wrap-Up Quiz 7
  • Focus: vertebrate circulatory, urinary, and reproductive anatomy; population growth and competition

Week 8: Ecosystem Sampling and Lab Wrap-Up

  • McGraw Hill Lab: Sampling Ecosystems – Biological Sampling
  • Blackboard: Final Lab Exam
  • Focus: ecological sampling, organism-environment relationships, and cumulative laboratory synthesis

Course Outline and Workload

You should expect to spend approximately 4-6 hours most weeks reviewing lab materials, completing virtual lab assignments, taking short Blackboard Lab Wrap-Up quizzes, and studying for the final lab exam. Some weeks may require additional time because this is an accelerated 8-week online laboratory course. Students should plan to work consistently throughout the week and check Blackboard and KCTCS email daily.

Weekly rhythm: Most weekly lab work is due on Monday at 11:59 p.m. Eastern time. The Syllabus Quiz is due Thursday of Week 1, and the Week 8 lab and Final Lab Exam are due during the final week (Thursday, August 6).

It is recommended that you complete weekly work in the following order:

  • Review the weekly module overview, checklist, and due dates in Blackboard.
  • Review any posted lab background materials, images, videos, or study supports.
  • Open the McGraw Hill Connect lab assignment from Blackboard.
  • Complete the assigned virtual lab activity or activities in Connect.
  • Complete the Blackboard Lab Wrap-Up quiz for the week.
  • Check Blackboard grades, feedback, announcements, and messages regularly.

Attendance, Late Work, and Make-Up Policy

Attendance Statement

Your attendance is determined by completion of assigned material. You are expected to access course materials online in Blackboard and check your KCTCS email account daily. You are responsible for all posted announcements, assignments, quizzes, and exams.

Because this is an accelerated 8-week online course, attendance begins with completion of the required Syllabus Quiz during the first week of class. Students who do not complete the required first-week attendance assignment may be dropped from the course without notification as per home campus procedures.

Late Assignments and Make-Up Policy

Assignments, McGraw Hill virtual lab assignments, Blackboard Lab Wrap-Up quizzes, and the Final Lab Exam are due on the dates listed in the course schedule. Students should use the weekly due dates to stay on pace in the course.

The Syllabus Quiz is due during the first week of class and does not use the general late-submission window. It is used as evidence of attendance and should be completed by the posted Start Here deadline.

General Late-Submission Window

Except for the Syllabus Quiz, the Week 8 lab assignment, and the Final Lab Exam, most weekly coursework remains available through Thursday, August 6 at 11:59 p.m. Eligible McGraw Hill virtual lab assignments and Blackboard Lab Wrap-Up quizzes may be submitted after the original weekly due date during this late-submission window without contacting me.

The weekly due dates are still the expected completion dates. The late-submission window is intended to provide flexibility for unexpected events, scheduling conflicts, work obligations, minor illnesses, family responsibilities, and routine technical difficulties while helping students remain on pace with the course.

After the late-submission window closes, assignments will close and no additional submissions will generally be accepted. Because all students receive the same built-in late-submission opportunity, individual requests to reopen assignments are generally not granted.

End-of-course assignments: The Week 8 lab assignment and the Final Lab Exam must be completed by the posted final course deadline. These items do not use the general late-submission period because the course ends that week.

If a significant emergency or extended absence affects your ability to complete coursework, please contact me as soon as possible to discuss available options.

Withdrawal Policy

A student may officially withdraw from any class up to and including the date of midterm with a W grade. After the date of midterm and through the last class of the semester or session, any student may officially request to withdraw from a course and receive a W, which may be given at the discretion of the instructor.

Withdrawal requests will only be approved if the student has continued working in class and at least 80% of the coursework to that date is completed with a grade more than 0. An instructor shall not assign a student a W for a class unless the student has officially withdrawn from that class in a manner prescribed by the college. The grade of W may be assigned by the College Appeals Board in classes involving a violation of student academic rights or for academic offenses. Please refer to the Student Records Office for more information.

Academic Integrity

Students must complete their own work. Academic dishonesty will not be tolerated. A first offense will result in a failing grade on the assignment. A second offense will result in a failing grade for the course.
